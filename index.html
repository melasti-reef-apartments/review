01

Introduction

We rented apartment 4A at Melasti Reef (Ungasan, Bali) through the Suite Stay agency for one month: from January 17 to February 17, 2026. The price was 18,000,000 IDR/month + a 5,000,000 IDR security deposit.

We had been living in Bali for 11 months and moved between several places. Minor issues happened everywhere — that's normal. But in every previous place, managers or owners solved problems quickly: you send a message, they reply, the issue gets fixed. Here, it was completely different. The main problem is not the building itself — it is the completely useless management. You pay your money and are left totally helpless: there is no one on the property you can talk to in person. The only way to communicate is a WhatsApp group chat with five staff members who systematically ignore messages, reply with copy-paste apologies, and do nothing to fix anything.

When we asked for a refund and to move out — we were ignored. When we demanded the contact details of someone in charge — we were ignored. The deposit was returned late and only after we threatened to publish reviews. Below is a detailed, fact-based report.

02

Timeline of events

Every problem dragged on for days and weeks. We received endless apologies, but nothing was actually done. Below is a timeline of our interactions with the management team, which included 5 people: Intan, Nia, Celine, Aprille, Niel.

January 18 (Day 2)
New issue

No hot water and very low water pressure in the shower

From the very first day, the shower only ran for about 2 minutes before water pressure dropped to almost nothing and hot water stopped completely. It was impossible to take a normal shower. We reported this in the chat. Response: "we'll inform the technician."

January 19 (Day 3)

A technician came and said "there is no problem." Water pressure is "normal," they checked — "everything works." In reality, nothing changed.

January 22 (Day 6)

We sent a video of the shower problem again. We were simply ignored.

New issue

Terrible cleaning quality

After cleaning, the apartment had a strong musty smell because the cleaner used old dirty rags. Management claimed the smell was coming from our belongings. The cleaner came, but did almost nothing: construction debris behind the bathroom door had been there since the apartment was built and stayed there for the entire month. Bed sheets were changed only once during the whole month — and only after we personally asked.

January 23 (Day 7)
New issue

Water shut off without warning

Water was turned off for several hours. We were not warned in advance. Later they told us it was "scheduled maintenance."

January 24 (Day 8)
New issue

Ceiling leak in the bathroom with a strong urine smell

Water started dripping from the bathroom ceiling. It had a strong urine smell. The leak was dripping directly onto the toilet and the floor, making the bathroom almost impossible to use. Water was also dripping near electrical wiring, creating a risk of electric shock.

New issue

No internet

The internet stopped working. We reported it immediately with a photo of the router. For remote workers, this is critical.

January 25 (Day 9)

We sent a long message listing all unresolved problems and tagged all 5 group members. Celine replied with a copy-paste apology.

January 26–27 (Days 10–11)

Internet has been down for 3 days. We ask them to call the provider. The provider schedules a repair for Thursday (January 29). A water pump issue is also discovered.

January 30 (Day 14)

The internet technician finally arrives — after 6+ days with no internet.

New issue

Plaster falling from the ceiling

A worker patched the ceiling — the patch fell off within a few hours. Heavy pieces of plaster were falling right on top of us. When we asked them to urgently send the workers back, they said "their schedule is full" and they would come tomorrow. Plaster is falling on your head, and management thinks it can wait.

January 31 (Day 15)

The workers never came back. We write: "Why didn't they come? We can't use the toilet properly — water with a urine smell is dripping from the ceiling." Response: "We apologize, hopefully tomorrow."

February 1 (Day 16)

The ceiling is swollen with water, puddle on the floor. Workers come — and leave without doing anything.

February 2 (Day 17)

We ask for a refund and to move out for the first time: "We want to move out. Please return our money and we will relocate to another place. Living here is unbearable." The refund request was completely ignored. Workers come and say they need 2 more days for repairs.

February 3 (Day 18)

We tag and ping all 5 staff members multiple times. We try calling — no one picks up. We calculate the refund amount: 12,800,000 IDR and send it to the chat. Our refund request is again completely ignored.

February 4 (Day 19)

Intan responds for the first time in 3 days (says she was in the hospital). She explains it is a "structural issue from the original developer." The refund question is completely ignored — not a single word about our request to move out and get our money back.

February 5–6 (Days 20–21)

The temporary fix doesn't work. The leak continues. We ask for the contact details of someone who can actually resolve this — we are ignored again. From February 6 on, no one responds to our messages at all.

February 6 — February 16

10 days of complete silence from the management team. The leak was eventually fixed about 5 days before our checkout, but our refund request was never addressed.

Deposit return: a separate story

February 16

We notify them we are checking out tomorrow. They tell us the deposit will be returned within 14 business days (not on checkout day, as is normally expected).

February 17

We check out. We provide our BNI bank details. They promise to return the deposit within 10–14 business days.

March 1 (12 days later)

No money received. We message them — silence.

March 2 (13 days later)

Intan replies: "Apologies for the delay, let me follow up to our finance."

March 7 (18 days later)

Still no money. We write again — both in the private chat and in the group.

March 8 (19 days later)

We warn them: if the deposit is not returned, we will publish reviews on all platforms.

March 10 (21 days later)

The deposit is finally returned — only after we threatened to publish reviews. Instead of the promised 10–14 days, it took 21 days.

Not just us: our neighbors from the Netherlands

Our upstairs neighbors — a couple from the Netherlands — had the same problems: water leaks, dirty rooms, and other issues that management refused to fix. They came to our apartment two or three times to talk about it — we were surprised how similar our experiences were.

They were the ones who warned us that they had found reviews from other tenants describing a pattern of unreturned deposits by this company. As it turned out later, our own experience confirmed this completely.

One day, during a work call, I could hear our neighbor walking around the courtyard, calling the manager on the phone, trying to get any kind of response about his problems. He was being ignored — just like us. He was on the verge of a breakdown. This clearly shows that our case is not unique: the system is set up in a way that leaves tenants completely alone with their problems, with no way to change anything.

06

Conclusion

The main problem with Suite Stay is not the building itself — it's that once you pay, you are completely on your own.

There is no one from the management company on-site. You cannot walk up to someone, explain your problem, or demand a solution in person. The only way to reach them is a WhatsApp group chat where five staff members take turns writing "sorry for the inconvenience" and do absolutely nothing. You can be ignored for hours and days. You write — silence. You call — no one picks up.

During our one month stay, we dealt with a ceiling leak that took 19 days to fix; a shower with no proper water pressure; 6 days with no internet; and plaster falling on our heads. When we asked for a refund and to move out — our request was simply ignored. We had to stay until the end of the lease in unbearable conditions.

After checkout, they promised to return the deposit within 10–14 business days. In reality, it took 21 days, and the deposit was returned only after we threatened to publish reviews. Our neighbors had warned us that they found reviews from other tenants describing a pattern of unreturned deposits by this company.

Avoid Suite Stay and Melasti Reef Apartment at all costs

We genuinely love Bali — we have been living here for nine months — so we say this objectively: the island is beautiful, but this place is the exact opposite. If we had come here as regular tourists, we would have never wanted to return to Bali. Our goal is not just to leave a review, but to warn future travelers so they don't make the same mistake.

All facts in this review are supported by WhatsApp conversations and photographs.
